Vocabulary and Text Organizing

29 Sep 2016 on Lexical, Teaching

More Classifications of Words to Talk About Words

My book is talking about “closed systems” versus “open systems” in lexis. There are words which are grammar words and words that are lexical words.

Grammar words: and, to, the, in, this…

Lexical words: monkey, sculpture, temperance, aboriginal…

And then it turns out there’s also a secret class of words called discourse-organizing words. These are words like issue, problem, assessment that help us frame how we think about the discourse AROUND them.

They’re both super useful and pose problems for language learners. A student who has trouble with discourse-organizing words may be disadvantaged in the struggle to decode whole texts as efficiently or accurately as possible.

Modality Is Also Cool

Modality is usually thought to be the province of modal verbs such as can, should, might etc. But actually, based on the research, a lot more lexical items are used to express modality:

Apparently, assume, doubt, guess, as if, suggest, think, actually, surprisingly

All these words help us ascertain the attitude of the speaker/writer. While the actual modal auxiliary verbs are fairly frequently taught in classrooms, the REST of the words expressing modality are given far less time and attention.

Innnnnnteresting.
