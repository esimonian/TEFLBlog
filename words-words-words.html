Words Words Words

15 Sep 2016 on Lexical, Teaching

Meta Terminology

Words about words.

Collocations are sort of cheat codes for words. We know that “miss the” goes more often with “bus”, “train”, than “lose the” does.

Homonyms are words that share the same form but have unrelated meanings. Spelled and pronounced the same, but mean different things.

Homophones are pronounced the same but spelled differently (and have different meanings).

Homographs are written the same but pronounced differently (and have different meanings).

Polysemes are words that are MOSTLY different but still have related meanings. Like lift (v) and lift (British n).

Synonyms and antonyms have similar or opposite meanings.

Hyponyms are basically one class up. Hammer, saw, paintbrush are all hyponyms of tool.

Meronyms are words of the same class in hyponyms. The hyponym of red, yellow, blue is color, and the colors themselves are all meronymous.
